 Base Rate, Allowable Spread, and Allowable Variance ( 13 CFR § 120.214 ): 
i. A loan may have a variable interest rate. The base rate may be one of the 
following:  
a) The Prime Rate; or 
b) The SBA Optional Peg rate.  
ii. The allowab le spread is based on the loan  amount . 
a) Loans of $50,000 and less : Base rate + 6.5% ; 
b) Loans of $50,001 up to and including $250,000: Base rate + 6%; 
c) Loans of $250,001 up to and including $350,000: Base rate + 4.5%;  
d) Loans of $350,001 and higher : Base rate + 3%. 
iii. The Lender must designate on its application for guaranty the amount of the 
percentage spread to be added to the base rate at each adjustment date.  
 Policy on Variable Interest Rates  
i. Standard Policy:  
SBA’s maximum allowable interest rate applies only to the initial Note rate 
on a variable rate loan. Subsequent changes in the base rate are not  subject to 
the maximum rate at the time of loan application ; however, the maximum 
spread over the base cannot exceed SBA’s stated maxim um.
