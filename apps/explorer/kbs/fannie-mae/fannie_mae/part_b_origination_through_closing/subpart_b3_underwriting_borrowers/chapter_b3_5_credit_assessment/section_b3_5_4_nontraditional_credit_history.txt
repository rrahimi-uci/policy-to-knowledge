Overview
If one or more borrowers do not have a credit score or have a limited traditional credit history, the lender must
establish an acceptable nontraditional credit history. The lender must ﬁrst check all three major credit
repositories to verify the borrower’s credit history.
The credit report will indicate if a credit score could not be produced due to insuﬃcient credit. Lenders must
ensure that the credit report accurately reﬂects the borrower’s information, such as the name, Social Security
number, and current residence of the borrower to conﬁrm that the lack of traditional credit was not erroneously
reported because incorrect information was used to order the credit report.
