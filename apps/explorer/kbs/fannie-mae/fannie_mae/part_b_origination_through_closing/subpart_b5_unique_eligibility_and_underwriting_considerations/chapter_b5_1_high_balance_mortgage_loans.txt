Loan Limits
The high-balance loan requirements apply to mortgage loans with original loan amounts meeting the high-cost
area loan limits established by the Federal Housing Finance Agency. Fannie Mae publishes on its website the
maximum high-cost area loan limits that may apply by state (or territory); however, speciﬁc loan limits are
established for each county (or equivalent) and may be lower for each speciﬁc high-cost area. Refer to Loan
Limits for Conventional Mortgages  for additional information, including the loan limits for each area.
Lenders are responsible for ensuring that the original principal balance of each mortgage loan does not exceed
the applicable maximum loan limit for the speciﬁc area in which the property is located. To assist lenders in
determining the applicable limits, Fannie Mae posts reference material on its website, including the Loan Limit
Geocoder, which lenders can use to look up loan limits based on a speciﬁc address (or batch of addresses).
