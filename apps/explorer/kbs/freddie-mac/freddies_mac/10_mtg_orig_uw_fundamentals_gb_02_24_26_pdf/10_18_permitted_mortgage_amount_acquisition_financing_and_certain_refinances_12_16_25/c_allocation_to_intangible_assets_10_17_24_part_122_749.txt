23.4 Tenant -Based Rental Assistance Vouchers (08/26/25)  
This form of subsidy provides rental assistance to a specific tenant rather than a specific property.  
Typically, t he Property receives a cash payment on behalf of the tenant from the subsidy provider 
based on the number of qualifying tenants.  This form of subsidy travels with the tenant and does 
not stay at the property.  The written authorization provided to the tenant for this subsidy is known 
as a voucher.  
 
a. Tenant -based vouchers; definitions ( 08/26/25) 
• Section 8 Tenant Based Housing Choice Vouchers – These tenant -based vouchers are 
administered locally by public housing agencies (PHAs).  The PHAs receive federal funds 
from HUD to administer the tenant -based voucher program.  Although part of HUDs Housing 
Choice Voucher Program , these are different from HUD property -based vouchers  in that the 
voucher travels with the tenant ; it does not stay with the property .  
 
• Other Tenant -Based Voucher Programs – These include any tenant -based voucher program 
for rental assistance other than HUD ’s Tenant Based Housing Choice Voucher program.  
 
b. Underwriting criteria for tenant -based vouchers  (08/26/25) 
If the Property has existing tenant -based voucher holders, Freddie Mac underwrites to the 
lower of the tenant -based voucher rent, market rent or restricted rents, if applicable, because 
of the voucher tenant’s ability to move from the Property.   Tenant -based voucher income that 
exceeds the restricted rents may be included in underwritten income at Freddie Mac’s 
discretion.   
 
23.5 Other Specific HUD Programs  - Section 236  (08/26/25) 
This section provides the requirements for a Property benefiting from Section  236 interest 
subsidies, also referred to as Interest Reduction Payments (IRPs).  For a Section  236 Mortgage to 
be eligible for sale to Freddie Mac, the Property must be currently  receiving IRP assistance and 
must be in compliance with all applicable legislative and regulatory requirements.  
 
1. Types of Section 236 interest subsidies  
Freddie Mac purchases or credit enhances both types of Section 236 interest subsidies:  
 
• Section 236(b) Absorption  
 
An approved State or local lender purchases the insured Section  236 Mortgage, terminates 
the FHA Mortgage insurance, but keeps the Mortgage and IRP in place and provides 
additional financing if necessary.  
 
• Section 236(e)(2) Decoupling
