Guide Chapter 31 – Insurance Requirements  
The following are maximum deductibles allowed for flood insurance policies:  
 
For first -layer building coverage:  
 
• $50,000 per building for a Property with 10 buildings or less located in SFHAs  
 
• $500,000 per occurrence for a Property with more than 10 buildings located in SFHAs  
 
For Business Income/Rental Value coverage:  
 
• 15 day waiting period when expressed as a time -based deductible  
 
• $100,000 per occurrence when expressed as a monetary deductible  
 
When NFIP policies are used as part of the coverage, the maximum deductible available 
under the NFIP for the type of building being insured is acceptable.  
 
e. Seller/Servicer flood zone monitoring responsibilities ( 12/16/25) 
The Seller/Servicer must have a process in place that allows it to  monitor any FEMA NFIP 
map changes and  identify whether any buildings at the Property are newly designed in an 
SFHA.  Flood insurance is required for all buildings located in SFHAs per this chapter.  
 
For newly -designated buildings, flood insurance is to be in place no later than 120 days after 
the effective date of the FEMA NFIP map change.   
 
For buildings no longer located in SFHAs, flood insurance may be discontinued.  Within 10 
days of notifying the Borrower that flood insurance is required for additional buildings, or 
authorizing the Borrower to discontinue flood insurance coverage, the Ser vicer must give 
written notice to Freddie Mac via email at MF_Insurance_Compliance@freddiemac.com . The 
email must note the property name, loan number and the changes.  The Servicer must also 
complete and submit a Summary Update record in ICT with updated flood zone and flood 
coverage information.  The Summary Update must also include a copy of the LODR,  LOMA, 
or LOMR and any other applicable documentation.  
 
f. Flood insurance during FEMA map change application  (12/16/25) 
Borrowers may submit requests to FEMA for map/flood zone designation changes at any 
time during the loan.  The Servicer may remove a building from a SFHA o nly if the Servicer 
receives  an updated FEMA SFHD based  on any one of the following:  
 
• Letter of Map Amendment (LOMA ), or 
 
• Letter of Map Revision (LOMR), or  
 
• Letter of Determination Review (LODR)

coverage in amounts equal to the maximum available coverage from NFIP policies for the 
type of buildings insured .  The Borrower must provide a copy of the map change requests
