7.5 Prevention, detection, and reporting of Suspicious Activity and actual or p ossible 
Financial Crimes  (07/01/25)  
The revisions made to this section announced in the April 22, 2025 Bulletin are effective July 1, 2025. 
 
 
a. Generally (07/ 01/25)  
1. The Seller/Servicer must have comprehensive prevention, detection and reporting practices and procedures in place to address Suspicious Activity and actual or possible 
Financial Crimes in connection with Freddie Mac Matters.  Such practices and procedures 
must be subject to monitoring and controls that ensure the efficacy of such practices and 
procedures.  
 
2. The Seller/Servicer must take the following minimum steps to prevent and detect Suspicious Activity and actual or possible Financial Crimes in connection with Freddie 
Mac Matters:  
 
• Maintain a log of Suspicious Activity and actual or possible Financial Crimes involving 
any Freddie Mac Matters that have been reported to and/or investigated by the Seller/Servicer and reported in accordance with this chapter.  The log should specify 
the date on which such matter was identified internally and the date on which such 
matter was reported to Freddie Mac.  
 
• Provide the log to Freddie Mac no later than January 31
st of each year, for 
reconciliation of any Suspicious Activity or actual or possible Financial Crimes that 
have been reported to and/or investigated by Seller/Servicer during the prior calendar 
year, and otherwise upon Freddie Mac’s request.  The log should be sent to the 
Freddie Mac Multifamily Financial Crimes Mailbox , with a copy to Multifamily 
Counterparty Risk & Compliance via email at Multifamily_Eligibility@freddiemac.com .
