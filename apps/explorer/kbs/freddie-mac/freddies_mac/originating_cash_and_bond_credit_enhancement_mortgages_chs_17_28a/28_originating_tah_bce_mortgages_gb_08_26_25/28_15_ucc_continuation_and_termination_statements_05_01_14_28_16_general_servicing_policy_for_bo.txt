b. Bond rating and bond opinion (02/29/12)  
Prior to executing the bond credit enhancement agreement, Freddie Mac must receive  
 
1. A rating letter or other evidence satisfactory to Freddie Mac indicating that the bonds, when 
issued, will bear a rating by Standard & Poor’s Ratings Services or Moody’s Investors 
Service that is acceptable to Freddie Mac.  
 
2. An unqualified opinion, satisfactory to Freddie Mac, from a nationally recognized bond 
counsel as to  
 
• The authorization and valid issuance of the bonds  
 
• The validity of the lien of the indenture  
 
• The excludability from gross income, for federal income tax purposes, of the interest 
payable on the bonds  
 
• Freddie Mac’s right to rely upon the opinion, or alternatively, a reliance letter addressed 
to Freddie Mac giving Freddie Mac the right to rely upon the opinion of the bond counsel  
 
 UCC continuation and termination statements (05/01/14)  
The Seller’s responsibility for filing UCC  (Uniform Commercial Code) continuation and termination 
statements applies only to financing statements under which Freddie Mac is the secured party, and 
does not apply to financing statements under which the bond issuer or the bond trustee is the only 
secured party.  With respect to any financing statement under which Freddie Mac is the secured 
party, the Seller must not file a termination statement until the bond credit enhancement agreement 
has terminated and all  obligations of the Borrower under the reimbursement and security agreement 
have been satisfied.  See Section  29.4 and the Final Delivery Instructions found at 
mf.freddiemac.com/lenders/purchase  for further information about UCC financing statements.  
 
 General Servicing policy for bond credit enhancements ( 08/26/25) 
Unless otherwise stated in the Commitment or this chapter, the Servicing of each bond credit 
enhancement must meet the requirements of Chapters  36 and 38 through  43. 
 
a. Servicing Spread ( 08/26/25) 
The Servicer’s servicing fee for each Mortgage will be the Servicing Spread set forth in the 
Servicing Spreads Chart  posted on our website and will be stated in the Purchase Contract for 
that Mortgage. If there is a difference between the Servicing Spread set forth in the Servicing 
Spreads Chart and the Servicing Spread set forth in the Purchase Contract , the Purchase 
Contract  will govern.
