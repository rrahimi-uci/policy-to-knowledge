11.8 Sponsor Meetings  (12/16/25)  
The provisions of this section apply to Mortgages that are placed under Seller  Application on or 
after February  1, 202 6, or any  Borrower consent request involving a  Transfer of Ownership 
submitted for approval on or after February 1, 2026, where the Mortgage or the Transfer of 
Ownership , as applicable, involves any of the following :  
• One or more of the following sponsor types (as a transferee, with respect to a Transfer of 
Ownership) :  
 
o First-Time  Sponsor ,  
o Limited Multifamily Experience Sponsor  (or for SBL Mortgages, Key Borrower Principals 
that do not individually meet the criteria noted  in Section 9SBL .2(c)(2)),  
o Rapid Growth Spons or  
• A broker/correspondent   
 
A Seller  Representative (as defined below) must meet with a Sponsor Representative (as defined 
below), either in person or by video call where the Sponsor Representative can be seen  (“Sponsor 
Meeting”).   The Sponsor Meeting must  take place within the 12 months preceding the submission 
of the full underwriting package , or full Transfer of Ownership package with respect to a Transfer 
of Ownership,  to Freddie Mac.   For the purposes hereof, (A) a “Sponsor Representative” means (i) 
any Key Borrower Principal that is an individual, or (ii) with respect to  a Key Borrower Principal 
that is an entity, an individual that is an officer, equity holder, or employee of a Key Borrower 
Principal with significant responsibility to Control or direct matters relating to the Key Borrower 
Principal, the Mortgage or the Property  and (B) a “Seller Representative” means an individual

If a transaction involves multiple sponsors where one or more of the sponsors meet s the criteria 
noted above, a Sponsor Meeting must be conducted with a Sponsor Representative for each such 
sponsor , but is not required for sponsors who do not meet the criteria noted above .  If there are 
multiple sponsors that the meet the criteria noted above, the Seller can conduct a joint meeting or 
separate meetings.  
 
If a transaction involves a broker/correspondent and there are no sponsors meeting the criteria 
noted above, then the Sponsor Meeting may be conducted with any Sponsor Representative.   
 
During the Sponsor Meeting, the Seller  must, at a minimum:  
 
• Confirm the identity of the Sponsor Representative using a current driver’s license or passport 
and the Sponsor  Representative ’s personal address.  
 
• Ask the Sponsor Representative to describe the individual’s role with respect to the Key 
Borrower Principal (if the Key Borrower Principal is an entity)  
 
• Ask the Sponsor Representative about their professional background and business objectives  
 
The Seller’s  mortgage transaction narrative analysis , or Transfer of Ownership/Assumption 
request form in the case of a Transfer of Ownership, must include a description of the Sponsor 
Meeting that includes the following:    
 
• The name, company and title of each participant  
 
• The date on which the Sponsor Meeting took place  
 
• Whether the Sponsor Meeting took place by video call, or if in person, the location in which the 
Sponsor Meeting took place  
 
• A summary of the  document ation  used by the Seller to confirm the identity of the Sponsor 
Representative  
 
• The role of the Sponsor Representative with respect to the Key Borrower Principal  
 
• A summary of the matters discussed  
 
• Any other information learned during the Sponsor Meeting that is likely to be material to 
Freddie Mac in its underwriting or approval of the transaction  
 
Unless requested by Freddie Mac, no documentation other than the mortgage transaction 
narrative analysis , or Transfer of Ownership/Assumption request form in the case of a Transfer of 
Ownership , is required regarding the Sponsor Meeting.  The Seller should not submit to Freddie 
Mac copies of  driver’s licenses or passports.
