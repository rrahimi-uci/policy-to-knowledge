Guide Chapter 60 – Appraiser and Appraisal 
Requirements  
The appraiser must provide rents, property taxes, operating expenses, and capitalization rates 
data from properties that have recently experienced renovations similar to the subject or are 
comparable to the proposed renovated Property.  If these benchmarks  are not available in the 
local market, the appraiser must explain the extent of research to obtain this type of data and 
provide an adequate discussion about how these items might impact the value from the as -is 
market value scenario.  
 
The appraiser must provide separate sales and rent comparables for the as -is market value and 
the hypothetical as -if renovated and stabilized current market value, as the renovation might 
promote the Property into a higher class of property.  
 
60.29  Appraisals for Mortgages with a Utility Efficiency Loan option (1 2/16/25 ) 
If the Seller/Servicer requests a Prospective As -Improved Market Value, the Appraisal must 
include both an As -Is Market Value and Prospective As -Improved Market Value.  
 
• The As -Is Market Value is the market value of the Property, assuming no Utility Efficiency  
Improvements have been completed.  Standard appraisal approaches and industry best 
practice per this chapter apply.  This value provides a baseline to determine how much 
additional value the Utility Efficiency  Improvements might generate.  
 
• The Prospective As -Improved Market Value must assume that all Utility Efficiency  
Improvements are completed as of the current date of value.  
 
To develop the Prospective As -Improved value, the appraiser must review the list of Utility 
Efficiency  Improvements that the Borrower has committed to execute at the Property, and 
consider the contribution of these Utility Efficiency  Improvements to the Property’s prospective 
market value.  The appraiser should not merely add the projected cost of the Utility Efficiency  
Improvements to the As -Is value and consider this a complete valuation.  
 
The appraiser must consider how rents, expenses and the capitalization rate will be affected by 
the Utility Efficiency  Improvements and the time it will take to implement the Utility Efficiency  
Improvements.  
 
The appraiser must reference Form  1106, Green Assessment , including  the date of the report and 
a summary of the report’s conclusions and recommendations.  
 
Freddie Mac prefers that the Prospective As -Improved Market Value be supported by comparable 
sales that also had a similar scope of Utility Efficiency  Improvements as well as by the appraiser’s 
survey of investors, lenders, property owners and/or brokers familiar with capitalization rates and 
investor considerations of Utility Efficiency  properties.  
 
In addition, Freddie Mac recommends that the appraiser complete the “Valuation of Sustainable 
Buildings – Commercial” professional development program offered through the Appraisal 
Institute.  A registry of appraisers who have completed this training can b e found at the Appraisal 
Institute’s website.

60.30  Appraisals for Forward Commitment Mortgages ( 10/21/25 ) 
1. For a Forward Commitment Mortgage  for affordable housing properties  and Targeted 
Affordable Housing  (as noted in Section  60.24) , the Appraisal must include the following 
valuation types:  
 
a. As-Is current market value  
b. Hypothetical  market value  as if complet ed and stabilized today with current restricted rents  
c. Hypothetical market value as if completed and stabilized today with out restricted rents  
(based on market rents)  
d. Prospective market value upon completion  
e. Prospective market value upon completion without restricted rents (based on market rents)  
f. Prospective market value upon completion and stabilization  
g. Prospective market value upon completion and stabilization without restricted rents (based 
on market rents)  
h. All other requirements of Chapter 60  
 
2. For all other Forward Commitment Mortgages, the Appraisal must include the following 
valuation types:  
 
a. As Is current market value  
b. Hypothetical market value as if completed and stabilized today with current market rents  
c. Prospective market value upon completion  
d. Prospective market value upon completion and stabilization  
e. All other requirements of Chapter 60  
 
3. A new or updated Appraisal will not be required at Conversion for a Forward Commitment 
Mortgage except if:  
 
a. A request has been made for a loan amount in excess of the original committed amount, 
or 
b. The appraisal is dated more than 48 months prior to the date the Conversion package is 
submitted.  
60.31  Ground leases ( 12/12/24) 
Freddie Mac purchases Mortgage loans on the leasehold interest in a subject property; i.e., the 
ownership interest receiving the monthly rent from the property’s tenants.  It is important, then, 
that the appraiser adequately model the financial impact of the ground lease payments on the  
multifamily operations component and consider all aspects of the valuation risk associated with 
that ground lease on the subject property.  
 
a. Valuation conclusions ( 12/12/24) 
At a minimum, Freddie Mac requires the appraiser provide an as-is estimate of the leasehold 
market value of the subject Property on the date of value ( See Section 60. 1(b)).   
 
If the Ground Lessor will not join in the Mortgage ( unsubordinated ground lease), the 
appraiser must also provide an as -is estimate of the leased fee market value of the subject 
Property on the date of value.
