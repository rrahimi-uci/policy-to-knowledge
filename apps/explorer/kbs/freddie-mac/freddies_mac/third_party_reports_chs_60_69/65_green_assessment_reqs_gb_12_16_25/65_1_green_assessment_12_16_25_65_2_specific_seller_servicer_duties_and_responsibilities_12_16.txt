Guide Chapter 65 – Green  Assessment  Requirement s 
 Green Assessment (1 2/16/25 ) 
This chapter sets forth the requirements, duties and responsibilities of the Seller/Servicer and the 
Utility Efficiency  Consultant to provide a Green Assessment.   
 
The purpose of the Green Assessment  is to: 
 
• Analyze current energy and water consumption  
 
• Evaluate how the building is operated and identify measures to increase operational efficiencies  
 
• Provide an ENERGY STAR® Energy Score as determined by Portfolio Manager®  
 
• Provide an EPA 1 -100 Water Score as determined by Portfolio Manager  
 
• Identify the energy conservation measures and water conservation measures from which the 
Borrower will select the Utility Efficiency  Improvements  
 
A Borrower must inform the Seller/Servicer if the Borrower is interested in obtaining a Green 
Assessment .  The Borrower must select qualifying conservation measures from among those 
identified by the Utility Efficiency  Consultant in the Green Assessment  before Freddie Mac will 
confirm that the Property is eligible for a Utility Efficiency Loan .  The conservation measures selected 
by the Borrower will be documented as Utility Efficiency  Improvements in the Utility Efficiency  
Improvement Rider to the Loan Do cuments.  
 
In addition to obtaining a Green Assessment , the Seller/Servicer must also obtain other applicable 
third-party  reports (Appraisal, property condition report, seismic risk assessment report) as required 
by the Guide.  
 
 Specific Seller/Servicer duties and responsibilities ( 12/16/25 ) 
a. The Seller/Servicer must select, retain and direct the Utility Efficiency  Consultant.  
 
1. Prior to retaining or employing a Utility Efficiency  Consultant, the Seller/Servicer must review 
at least three references from lenders who have retained or employed the Utility Efficiency  
Consultant to sufficiently evaluate the Utility Efficiency  Consultant’s capabilities and 
performance.  
 
2. The Borrower must not retain or direct the Utility Efficiency  Consultant.  However, the 
Borrower may be responsible for paying the costs of all Utility Efficiency  Consultant services 
above the reimbursement which Freddie Mac has agreed to pay upon Freddie Mac’s purchase 
of the Mortgage.  
 
3. The Seller/Servicer is accountable for the Utility Efficiency  Consultant’s performance.  
 
b. The Seller/Servicer must obtain, review and verify the Utility Efficiency  Consultant’s credentials, 
licensing, applicable certifications, as well as information related to affiliations or memberships in 
professional organizations.

c. The Seller/Servicer must maintain a separate Utility Efficiency  Consultant file for all Utility 
Efficiency  Consultants that includes the Seller/Servicer’s ongoing evaluations of each Utility 
Efficiency  Consultant’s performance, as well as the Utility Efficiency  Consultant’s current resume, 
required references and a copy of the current certificate(s) of liability insurance in accordance with 
the requirements of Section 11.5.  
 
d. The Seller/Servicer must provide the Utility Efficiency  Consultant with 13 months of utility 
consumption data for all owner paid utilities.  If tenants pay for utilities consumed in the units, the 
Seller/Servicer must obtain at least 10 percent of tenant consumption data.  In addition, the 
Seller/Servicer must  obtain all information pertaining to the maintenance, repair and replacement 
of major building components or systems before the Utility Efficiency  Consultant completes the 
Green Assessment . 
 
e. The Seller/Servicer must obtain a Green Assessment  for the Property, including  
 
• Reviewing the Green Assessment  to ensure that it complies with Freddie Mac’s requirements  
 
• Providing a copy of the Green Assessment  to the Borrower so that the Borrower may select 
conservation measures  
 
• Directing the Utility Efficiency  Consultant to share Property information with Freddie Mac in 
Portfolio Manager  
 
• Ensuring that the Borrower has a Portfolio Manager account and ensuring that the Utility 
Efficiency  Consultant has access to the Borrower account so that the Property data that is 
input by the Utility Efficiency  Consultant can be shared with the Borrower  
 
f. The Seller/Servicer must monitor completion of the Utility Efficiency  Improvements and 
compliance with the benchmarking requirements in Section 39.10 and the Loan Documents by 
ensuring that the Borrower  
 
• Includes funds for the completion of the Utility Efficiency  Improvements in compliance with the 
requirements in Chapter 39 and the Loan Documents  
 
• Completes the Utility Efficiency  Improvements within the timeline identified in the Loan 
Documents  
 
• Retains a Benchmarking Data Consultant that meets the requirements set forth on the 
Benchmarking Data Collection – Consultant Requirements document  located at 
mf.freddiemac.com  
 
• Reports on energy and water consumption through the Benchmarking Data Consultant as 
described in Section 39.10 and in the Loan Documents  
 
g. The Seller/Servicer must direct the Utility Efficiency  Consultant to include in the Green 
Assessment  the reliance provisions found in Section 65.3(a)(3)
