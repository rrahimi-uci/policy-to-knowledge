Guide Chapter 32 – Final Delivery Requirements  
 
Seller/Servicer must review and analyze the chain of title described above (including both the 
actual documents and any accompanying report from the Title Company ) and obtain Freddie 
Mac’s approval for any inconsistencies with the Underwriting Package, any evidence of non -
arms’ length transactions, or any other irregularities or inconsistencies that would be of 
material interest to a prudent lender.   
 
For acquisition Mortgage origination transactions, Seller/Servicer must also review the fully 
executed deed or other conveyance document reflecting the sale of ownership interests, as 
applicable, to confirm that such documents match the draft documents pre viously provided by 
Borrower, that the signature blocks align with the parties and signature blocks in the Purchase 
Agreement, and that such documents do not show any indication of an intervening, 
unexplained transfer or indication of any affiliation betwe en the property seller and Borrower 
that has not previously been disclosed and approved by Freddie Mac.  If such review reveals 
any inconsistencies  with the information  contained in the Purchase Agreement Analysis , 
Seller/Servicer must deliver an updated  Purchase Agreement Analysis  and obtain Freddie 
Mac’s approval of such updated analysis.   
 
32.4 Specific final delivery requirements ( 10/01/25 ) 
In addition to the general requirements set forth in this chapter, more specific delivery 
requirements are set forth in the Final Delivery Instructions  and the Purchase Contract . 
 
32.5 Delivery of Initial Delivery Packages for Forward Commitments for non -SBL 
Mortgages (09/01/16)  
For all Forward Commitment products, the Seller must submit the Initial Delivery Package to 
Multifamily Purchase  in accordance with the Final Delivery Instructions – Forward Commitment 
Initial Delivery.  Final Delivery Packages for all Forward Commitment products must be submitted 
in accordance with the Final Delivery Instructions – Cash and Bonds.

32.11  Preparing for purchase  (09/01/16)  
a. File identification prior to funding (05/01/14)  
When a Seller delivers a Mortgage to Freddie Mac for purchase, the Seller must note on the 
Loan Documents and accounting records retained in the Seller's file that Freddie Mac may 
purchase the Mortgage.  Because Freddie Mac may reject a Mortgage before purchase, the 
Seller must not transfer the Mortgage to a Freddie Mac portfolio designation until Freddie 
Mac has purchased the Mortgage.
