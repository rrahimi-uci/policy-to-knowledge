205 
Comment 
One commenter said that any changes 
need to take into account discrepancies between state and Federal laws regarding release of information and ways to protect patients from the consequences of their information being used against them. 
Response 
The Department acknowledges that 
the complex intersection of state and Federal behavioral health privacy 
statutes and regulations may result in unnecessary or improper disclosures. As we have noted in this section, part 2 does not preempt more stringent state statutes or regulations. Likewise, we have stated that HIPAA constitutes a floor of privacy protection that does not preclude more stringent state laws.
