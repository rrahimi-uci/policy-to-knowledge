════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
DEDUPLICATION PRINCIPLES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. **CONSERVATIVE APPROACH - When In Doubt, Keep Both**
   ❌ DO NOT merge if ANY of these differ:
   - Specific timeframes (60 days vs 72 hours vs 30 days)
   - Patient populations (Medicare vs Medicaid vs commercial)
   - Care settings (inpatient vs outpatient vs SNF)
   - Regulatory body (CMS vs TJC vs state)
   - Threshold values (≥500 individuals vs <500 individuals)
   - Provider types (physician vs facility vs pharmacist)

2. **IDENTIFY TRUE DUPLICATES**
   ✅ Rules ARE duplicates if ALL of these match:
   - Identical business requirement/constraint
   - Same timeframes and thresholds
   - Same regulatory context and scope
   - Same consequences for violation
   - Only differ in wording/phrasing
   - Total score ≥ 75 points

   Examples of TRUE duplicates:
   - "PHI breach ≥500 must be reported to HHS within 60 days" vs "HHS notification required within 60 days for breaches affecting 500+ individuals" (Score: ~95)
   - "H&P required within 24 hours of admission" vs "History and physical must be completed no later than 24 hours post-admission" (Score: ~96)

3. **PRIMARY RULE SELECTION PRIORITY**
   Priority 1: Completeness → Priority 2: Specificity → Priority 3: Source Authority → Priority 4: Mandatory Status
