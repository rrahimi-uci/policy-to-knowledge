{content}

CHUNKING PRINCIPLES:

1. **PRESERVE SEMANTIC COHERENCE**
   - Keep related rules, definitions, and procedures together
   - Don't split mid-topic or mid-rule
   - Maintain natural section boundaries (chapters, major headings, topic changes)
   - Keep eligibility requirement clusters intact — splitting borrower eligibility from property eligibility creates incomplete underwriting rules

2. **OPTIMIZE FOR DOWNSTREAM MORTGAGE PROCESSING**
   - Each chunk should contain complete, self-contained information
   - Include sufficient context for mortgage entity identification (Borrower, Loan, Property, Servicer, etc.)
   - Include complete rule descriptions with dollar limits, LTV ratios, DTI limits, and credit score floors
   - Preserve regulatory cites (e.g., Sample Guidelines Selling Guide section numbers, Example Policies Guide B3 references, FHA handbook section numbers, VA pamphlet chapters)
   - Keep cross-references to AUS (DU/LP) approval conditions intact

3. **SIZE OPTIMIZATION**
   - Target: 2000-4000 characters per chunk (optimal for LLM context)
   - Minimum: 1000 characters (avoid fragmentation)
   - Maximum: 5000 characters (prevent information overload)
   - Flexible: Extend beyond limits if needed to preserve semantic coherence (e.g., a multi-condition eligibility rule with LTV, DTI, and credit score requirements should not be split)

4. **MORTGAGE-SPECIFIC STRUCTURAL AWARENESS**
   - Identify document hierarchy (chapters → sections → subsections)
   - Recognize mortgage-specific section types:
     * Borrower eligibility (residency status, citizenship, employment type)
     * Credit underwriting requirements (FICO floors, tradeline requirements, credit event seasoning)
     * Income and employment documentation requirements
     * LTV / CLTV / HCLTV ratio limits and table lookups
     * DTI ratio limits (front-end / back-end)
     * Conforming loan limits (by county, by unit count)
     * Property eligibility and appraisal requirements
     * Mortgage Insurance (MI) requirements and cancellation rules
     * Agency program-specific rules (HomeReady, Home Possible, FHA 203(k), VA IRRRL)
     * ARM adjustment caps and index definitions
     * Escrow / impound account requirements
     * Servicing transfer and loss mitigation procedures
     * AUS (DU / LP / GUS) findings interpretation
   - Detect enumerated eligibility matrices, LTV tables, and income calculation examples
   - Note cross-references to Sample Guidelines Selling Guide, Example Policies Single-Family Guide, FHA Handbook 4000.1, VA Lender Handbook

5. **METADATA RICHNESS**
   - Provide descriptive titles that indicate mortgage content type (e.g., "Conventional Loan LTV Limits — Purchase vs. Refinance Matrix", "FHA Credit Score Minimums and MIP Rate Table")
   - Include section identifiers from the document (e.g., "Section B3-3.1-01", "HUD 4000.1 II.A.4")
   - Summarize key mortgage entities or underwriting requirements in each chunk
   - Identify document type (agency selling guide, underwriting manual, program matrix, compliance handbook, rate/fee schedule)
