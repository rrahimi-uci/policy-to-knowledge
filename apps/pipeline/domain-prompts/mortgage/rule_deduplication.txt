YOUR TASK (Agent 5 - Deduplication):

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
DUPLICATE SCORING CRITERIA (0-100 Scale)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Calculate a **similarity score** for each potential duplicate pair using these weighted criteria:

**1. Semantic Equivalence (40 points maximum)**
- Identical business requirement: 40 points
- Equivalent requirement with minor wording variation: 30-35 points
- Similar but contextually different: 15-25 points
- Related but distinct requirements: 0-10 points

**2. Numeric Precision Match (25 points maximum)**
- All thresholds/amounts/percentages identical: 25 points
- Minor differences (e.g., 620 vs 640): 10 points
- Significant differences: 0 points
- No numeric values to compare: 25 points (N/A - give full credit)

**3. Contextual Alignment (20 points maximum)**
- Identical contexts (loan type, property type, etc.): 20 points
- Similar contexts with overlap: 10-15 points
- Different contexts: 0-5 points

**4. Regulatory Source Consistency (10 points maximum)**
- Same regulatory section/reference: 10 points
- Related sections within same document: 5-8 points
- Different sources expressing same requirement: 3-5 points
- Conflicting sources: 0 points

**5. Consequence Alignment (5 points maximum)**
- Identical consequences for compliance/violation: 5 points
- Similar consequences: 3 points
- Different consequences: 0 points

**SCORING INTERPRETATION:**
- **90-100**: DEFINITE duplicates - merge immediately (HIGH confidence)
- **75-89**: LIKELY duplicates - merge with detailed rationale (MEDIUM-HIGH confidence)
- **60-74**: POSSIBLE duplicates - review carefully, prefer keeping separate (MEDIUM confidence)
- **Below 60**: NOT duplicates - keep as separate rules

**MERGE THRESHOLD: Only merge rules scoring ≥ 75 points**

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
DEDUPLICATION PRINCIPLES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. **CONSERVATIVE APPROACH - When In Doubt, Keep Both**
   ❌ DO NOT merge if ANY of these differ:
   - Specific numeric thresholds (620 vs 640 credit score, 80% vs 97% LTV)
   - Dollar amounts ($726,200 vs $1,089,300 conforming limits)
   - Timeframes (30 days vs 60 days, 12 months vs 24 months)
   - Percentages (43% DTI vs 50% DTI)
   - Contexts (conventional vs FHA vs VA vs USDA loans)
   - Property types (single-family vs 2-4 units vs condos vs co-ops)
   - Regulatory sections (B3-5.1-01 vs B3-5.1-02)
   - Approval authorities (DU vs manual underwriting)
   - Consequences (denial vs warning vs exception required)

2. **IDENTIFY TRUE DUPLICATES**
   ✅ Rules ARE duplicates if ALL of these match:
   - Identical business requirement/constraint (40 pts)
   - Same numeric thresholds and conditions (25 pts)
   - Same regulatory context and scope (20 pts)
   - Same consequences for violation (5 pts)
   - Only differ in wording/phrasing
   - **Total score ≥ 75 points**
   
   Examples of TRUE duplicates:
   - "Borrower credit score must be ≥ 620" vs "Credit score requirement: minimum 620" (Score: ~95)
   - "LTV cannot exceed 97%" vs "Maximum LTV is 97%" (Score: ~98)
   - "Documentation dated within 60 days" vs "Documents must be no older than 60 days" (Score: ~92)

3. **PRIMARY RULE SELECTION PRIORITY (Choose in order)**
   When merging duplicates, select primary rule based on these criteria:
   
   **Priority 1: Completeness (Highest Priority)**
   - Most comprehensive description
   - Most attributes/conditions documented
   - Most examples provided (2-3 examples better than 1)
   - Most detailed consequences and exceptions

   **Priority 2: Specificity**
   - Most specific numeric thresholds
   - Clearest conditions and constraints
   - Most actionable/testable language
   - Includes edge cases

   **Priority 3: Source Authority**
   - Official regulatory reference (B2-1-01) over general reference
   - Primary source over secondary interpretation
   - More recent regulation over older version

   **Priority 4: Mandatory Status**
   - mandatory=true preferred over mandatory=false
   - Core requirements over optional guidelines

   **Priority 5: Rule ID Convention**
   - If all else equal, lower numbered rule ID (BR_001 over BR_089)
   - Entity rules (BR_ENTITY_*) over relationship rules (BR_REL_*)

4. **MERGE STRATEGY - Information Preservation**
   For each duplicate group:
   - **Primary rule**: Selected using priority criteria above
   - **Enhanced description**: Combine unique details from ALL duplicates
   - **Merged examples**: Include best examples from all rules (keep 2-3 most diverse)
   - **Combined references**: Append all regulatory references (semicolon-separated)
   - **Preserved attributes**: Merge unique conditions, exceptions, consequences
   - **Audit trail**: Document which rules merged and why

5. **DOMAIN EXPERTISE CHECKS**
   - Recognize implicit context differences (e.g., "purchase" vs "refinance" rules)
   - Understand cascading requirements (one rule may be subset of another)
   - Identify special cases vs general rules
   - Consider temporal aspects (effective dates, sunset clauses)
   - Flag rules that seem similar but serve different compliance functions
