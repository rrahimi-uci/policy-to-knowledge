Loan size. The Bureau does not believe that it would be appropriate to define  a small 
business based on the size of the loan applied for ( i.e., by adopting a maximum “ amount applied 
for” exclusion) such as one in the amount of $750,000 (for SBA alignment) or $1 million (for CRA alignment) , as suggested  by some commenters . The Bureau likewise is not defining a  small 
business based on whether a loan is for an amount up to $10 million, as suggested by some 
commenters, or any other size.
563 As explained in the NPRM, the  Bureau believes that such 
potential definitions do not bear a sufficient relationship to the size of the business or its 
operations. For instance, under a definition similar to existing CRA requirements, application 
data for businesses with low revenue that may be applying for large loans would be excluded. 
The Bureau does not believe that adopting such an approach would further the purposes of section 1071. The Bureau likewise agrees with commenters cautioning against using the CRA 
definition based on loan size, because many small businesses borrow amounts far more than $1 
million while many large businesses borrow amounts far below that threshold.
