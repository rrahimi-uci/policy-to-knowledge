e) Default Interest Rates:  
The default interest rate is a change (increase) in the interest rate 
charged to the Borrower as a result of a failure to meet certain 
condit ions specified in the loan agreement.  
i) A Lender may charge a default interest rate if it does so for its 
similarly -sized , non-SBA guaranteed commercial loans, as long as the 
interest rate does not exceed the maximum interest rate permitted for 
Export Expres s loans .  
ii) The amount of interest SBA will pay to a Lender following default of 
an Export  Express loan is capped at the maximum interest rates for the 
Standard 7(a) loan program.  
ii. Policy on Variable Interest Rates  
a) Standard Policy : 
SBA’s maximum allowable int erest rate applies only to the initial Note 
rate on a variable rate loan. Subsequent changes in the base rate are not 
subject to the maximum rate at the time of loan application ; however, 
the maximum spread over the base cannot exceed SBA’s stated
