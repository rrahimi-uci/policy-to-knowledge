5. Blended rate option  
 
With the blended rate option, Freddie Mac underwrites the Mortgage using one note rate 
where the terms of the single note produce substantially the same blended UPB, interest 
rate and amortization schedule that would be produced using the bifurcation optio n 
described above .  The UPB of the Mortgage at the end of the Rental Assistance Contract  
must be no greater than 102 percent of the UPB had Freddie Mac used the bifurcation 
option.

owning Rental Assistance Contract  properties  
 
• Whether the Property is located in  a strong market with a low vacancy rate in

c. Rental Assistance Contract Transition Reserves (08/26/25) 
Freddie Mac may require the funding of a Transition Reserve for a Property with a  Rental 
Assistance Contract .  
 
1. Size 
 
The size of the Transition Reserve must equal or exceed six months of amortizing debt 
service, based on the proportion of units covered by the Rental Assistance Contract  to 
the total number of rental units.  Freddie Mac may require a higher Transition Reserve for 
a Property located in a weak market or if there are other underwriting concerns.  
 
2. Funding  
 
A Transition Reserve is generally not required for a Long -Term Rental Assistance 
Contract.   The reserve will not be required so long as all of the following conditions have 
been met:  
 
• Compliant Guarantor  financial Liquidity  
 
• Sponsor  owns at least five properties with Rental Assistance Contracts , including the 
Property, and has at least 10 years’ experience in affordable housing investment or 
development.

3. Release scenarios  
 
Freddie Mac may release the Transition Reserve upon written request from the Borrower 
and satisfaction of at least one of the following two scenarios and the additional 
conditions set forth below:  
 
Scenario 1 – all conditions must be met:  
• There are no Loan defaults at the subject Property.  
• The Rental Assistance  Contract has expired and will not be renewed.  
 
• The Property, at market rents, achieves the current program minimum DSCR.  
 
• The Property must be in satisfactory physical condition as determined by the most 
recent inspection conducted by the Optigo Lender.  
 
• The Property has already transitioned to operate as a nonsubsidized, market -rate 
rental Property  and has achieved 90 percent occupancy for a minimum of 90 
consecutive days . 
 
Scenario 2 – all conditions must be met:  
• There are no Loan defaults at the Property  
• The Borrower has secured a new fully executed Long Term Rental Assistance 
Contract . 
• The Property, with the new Long -Term Rental Assistance Contract  rents, achieves 
the current program minimum DSCR.  
• The Property must be in satisfactory physical condition as determined by the most 
recent inspection conducted by the Optigo Lender.  
 
• The Property has achieved 90  percent occupancy for a minimum of the 
90 consecutive days prior to the written release request.
