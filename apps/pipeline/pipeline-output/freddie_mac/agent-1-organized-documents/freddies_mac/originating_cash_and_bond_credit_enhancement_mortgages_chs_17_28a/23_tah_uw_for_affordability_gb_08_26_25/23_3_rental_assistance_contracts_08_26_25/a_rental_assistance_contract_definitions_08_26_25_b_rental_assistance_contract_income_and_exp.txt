The property receives a cash payment from the contract administrator, HUD or the public housing 
agency based on the number of qualifying tenants living in qualifying units.  The Rental Assistance 
Contract remains with the property and does not follow tenan ts to other properties.   
 
Examples of Rental Assistance Contracts include: U.S. Department of Housing and Urban 
Development ( HUD ) Section 8 PBRA or PBV Contracts, NYC 15/15 Project -Based Rental 
Assistance Contracts, or Long Term Subsidy Contracts issued by the District of Columbia Housing 
Authority.  
 
The nature of the Rental Assistance Contract will determine the applicable underwriting 
requirements.  Early in the underwriting process, the Optigo Lender  must inform Freddie Mac of 
the type of Rental Assistance Contract  on the Property.  If the level of rental assistance benefiting 
the Property via a Rental Assistance Contract  is not material to the Property’s income ( less than or 
equal to  20 percent of EGI), Freddie Mac may not underwrite the Mortgage using Rental 
Assistance Cont ract criteria.  
 
If the Property benefits from more than one type of Rental Assistance Contract , Freddie Mac 
requires the Mortgage to meet the underwriting requirements of each Rental Assistance Contract .  
Where underwriting requirements overlap, the Mortgage must be underwritten to comply with the 
most restrictive  rental assistance  standard.  
 
a. Rental Assistance Contract ; definitions ( 08/26/25) 
A Rental Assistance Contract  is considered a Long Term  Rental Assistance Contract  if: 
 
• The term of the Mortgage is less than or equal to 20 years and the term of the Rental 
Assistance Contract  is greater than or equal to the term of the Mortgage  
 
• The term of the Mortgage is greater than 20 years and the term of the Rental Assistance 
Contract  is greater than or equal to 20 years  
 
All other Rental Assistance Contracts  are considered Short Term Rental Assistance 
Contracts . 
 
Other definitions pertinent to  Rental Assistance Contracts  are as follows:  
 
• Above Market, which refers to Rental Assistance Contract  rents that are higher than the 
achievable market rent, also referred to as “ Market  Overhang”  
 
• At or Below Market, which refers to Rental Assistance Contract  rents that are equal to or 
lower than the achievable market rent  
 
b. Rental Assistance Contract income and expense requirements  (08/26/25) 
Freddie Mac underwrites to the lower of market rents, Rental Assistance Contract  rents, or  
restricted  rents, where applicable , and with consideration given to whether there is a Long 
Term Rental Assistance Contract  or Short Term Rental Assistance Contract  in place .

For a Property with a Long Term Rental Assistance Contract , Freddie Mac underwrites to 
the lower of the Rental Assistance Contract  rent or market rent as follows:  
 
i) If the Property passes the refinance test at market rents, then Freddie Mac may 
underwrite the Mortgage with an Above Market Rental Assistance Contract  rent, 
up to 110 percent of market rents, without  using the blended rate option described  
below , or  
 
ii) If the Property does not pass the refinance test at market rents regardless of the 
term of the loan, then Freddie Mac must underwrite the Mortgage using the 
bifurcation or blended rate option described  in Guide Sections 23.3(b) 4 -5 below.  
If bifurcated, the real estate component must pass the refinance test at market 
rents.  If the blended rate has been used, the full loan must pass the refinance 
test using the blended interest rate and blended amortization . 
 
2. Properties with Short Term Rental Assistance Contracts  
 
Freddie Mac will underwrite a Property with a Short Term Rental Assistance Contract  to 
the lowest of the Rental Assistance Contract  rent, market rent or restricted  rent, if 
applicable .  However, if the Rental Assistance Contract  rent is Above Market  and the 
Property is not subject to any other rent restrictions , depending on the characteristics of 
the Mortgage, Freddie Mac may underwrite the rents with Market  Overhang using the 
bifurcation option or blended rate option described below, and must pass the ref inance 
test at market rents regardless of the term of the loan.  
 
3. Maturity risk analysis  
 
If the Mortgage is underwritten with Market  Overhang, then the Mortgage must pass a 
refinance test  at market rent , regardless of the term of the Mortgage.  In determining the 
achievable market rents, Freddie Mac reviews the market evidence and the appraiser’s 
estimate of market rents to evaluate the rent levels achievable at the Property without a 
Rental Assistance Contract . 
 
4. Bifurcation option  
 
Requirements for the bifurcation option are as follows:  
 
• The real estate component ( restricted  or market) is underwritten by calculating the 
Property’s Net Operating Income (NOI) using the lower of restricted  or market rents 
and expenses.  Freddie Mac uses restricted  rents when the unit is subject to rent 
restrictions and uses the market rents only when the unit is not subject to rent 
restrictions.  
 
• The Market  Overhang component must be sized to a DCR of 1.00x and structured so 
that it self -amortizes over the term of the Rental Assistance Contract  period or the 
term of the Freddie Mac Mortgage, whichever is less.  The Market  Overhang 
component is underwritten using only the amount of additional income that the higher 
Rental Assistance Contract  rents would provide assuming the appropriate Rental 
Assistance Contract  vacancy.  No expenses are deducted from the Market  Overhang 
income.
