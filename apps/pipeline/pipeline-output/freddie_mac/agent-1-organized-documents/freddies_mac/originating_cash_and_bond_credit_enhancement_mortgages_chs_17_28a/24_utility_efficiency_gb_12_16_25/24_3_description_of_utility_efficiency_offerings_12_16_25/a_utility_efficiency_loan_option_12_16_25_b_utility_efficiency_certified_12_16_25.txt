Guide Chapter 24 – Freddie Mac Multifamily Utility Efficiency  
24.1 Utility Efficiency  definition and overview  (12/16/25) 
Utility Efficiency  is a suite of offerings for a Borrower who has made or  plans to make the 
Borrower’s Property more energy and water efficient.   The offerings are as follows:  
 
• Utility Efficiency  – a loan option that become s available when a Borrower commits to 
completing qualifying energy and water conservation measures identified in a Green  
Assessment  
 
• Utility Efficiency  Certified – a loan option available when the Borrower’s Property has a Utility 
Efficiency  Building Certificate, as described in Section 55.2  
 
• C-PACE Consent – Freddie Mac’s consent to the Borrower’s pursuit of C -PACE financing  
 
The Borrower may elect to pursue only one of the Utility Efficiency  offerings , and the related 
Mortgage must meet the requirements for such offering  set forth in this chapter . 
 
24.2 Eligibility for Utility Efficiency  offerings  (12/16/25) 
a. Utility Efficiency  (12/16/25) 
• Conventional Cash Mortgages and TAH Cash Preservation Mortgages are eligible for 
Utility Efficiency . 
 
• The Mortgages can be fixed -rate or floating -rate, with  a 5-year, 7-year or  a 10-year term .  
 
SBL Mortgages, MHC Mortgages , Seniors Housing Mortgages, Supplemental Mortgages, 
and Student Housing  Mortgages are not eligible for Utility Efficiency . 
 
b. Utility Efficiency  Certified ( 12/16/25) 
Conventional Cash Mortgages and TAH Cash Preservation Mortgages that meet the 
affordability criteria  provided in the term sheet  are eligible for Utility Efficiency  Certified.  
 
c. C-PACE Consent (1 2/16/25 ) 
The Seller/Servicer must contact its Freddie Mac account manager to determine Mortgage 
eligibility for C -PACE Consent.  
 
24.3 Description of Utility Efficiency  offerings  (12/16/25 ) 
a. Utility Efficiency  Loan option (12/1 6/25) 
The process for using the Utility Efficiency  Loan option is as follows:  
 
• The Utility Efficiency  Consultant prepares a Green Assessment  as described in Chapter 
65. 
 
• The Green Assessment  identifies energy and water conservation measures.

• The Borrower commits to complete sufficient energy and/or water improvements from 
the measures listed in the Green Assessment  to result in a projected minimum energy or 
water consumption savings of 30 percent based on whole building consumption, 
provided that a minimum 15 percent of savings must be in energy consumption 
(Minimum Consumption Savings Threshold).  
 
• Borrower must retain a Benchmarking Data Consultant for the life of the loan to collect, 
input and monitor the energy and water usage in accordance with Section 39.10.   
 
• The energy and water improvements that the Borrower commits to complete will be 
identified as Utility Efficiency  Improvements in the Utility Efficiency  Improvements Rider 
to the Loan Agreement.  
 
• The Borrower must escrow 125 percent of the estimated cost of the Utility Efficiency  
Improvements as part of the Repair Reserve.  
 
• Work on the Utility Efficiency  Improvements must be completed within two years, with 
the exception of Utility Efficiency  Improvements for Value -Add Mortgages, Mod Rehab 
and Preservation Rehab Mortgages, which must be completed in accordance with 
Freddie Mac’s requirements for those products.  
 
To qualify for the Utility Efficiency  Loan option, the Property must meet all eligibility 
requirements as determined by Freddie Mac, including the Minimum Consumption Savings 
Threshold.   The Seller/Servicer must contact its Freddie Mac account manager for all 
eligibility criteria.  The Borrower, through the Benchmarking Data Consultant, will be 
required to provide Benchmarking Metrics to Freddie Mac on an annual basis.   The 
responsibilities of the Borrower, Benchmarking Data Consultant and the Servicer with  
respect to the benchmarking process are found in the Utility Efficiency  Improvements Rider 
attached to the Loan Agreement.  
 
Utility Efficiency  Improvements are considered to be repairs for monitoring and reporting 
purposes.   See Section 39.4.  Additional Servicing requirements relating to Utility Efficiency  
Improvements are found in Section 39.10.  
 
b. Utility Efficiency  Certified ( 12/16/25 ) 
A Fixed -rate Mortgage with a 10 -year term that is secured by a Property with a Utility 
Efficiency  Certificate may be considered  Utility Efficiency  Certified if 50 percent of the 
Property’s units are affordable at the AMI based on the market, as calculated using the 
Freddie Mac Affordability Test , located at https://mf.freddiemac.com/lenders/uw/affordability -
resources  and the Borrower delivers to the Seller a copy of the Utility Efficiency  Certificate 
for delivery by the Seller/Servicer in the underwriting package.
