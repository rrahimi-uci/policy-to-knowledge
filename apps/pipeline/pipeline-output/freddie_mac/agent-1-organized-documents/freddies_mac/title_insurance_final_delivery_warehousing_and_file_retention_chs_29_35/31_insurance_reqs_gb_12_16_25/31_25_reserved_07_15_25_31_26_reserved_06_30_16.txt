31.27  Captive Insurance  and risk retention groups  (RRG)  (12/16/25) 
The use of Captive insurance  or risk retention group s (RRG s) is acceptable for the following:  
 
• General and Professional Liability coverage,  including umbrella or excess  
 
• Property and Liability policy deductibles  
 
• Property coverage ; however , the Captive may not provide the majority of the coverage  
 
• Terrorism policies when backed by the Terrorism Risk Insurance Program  
 
The Seller/Servicer must review the  Captive or RRG to ensure the entity or its insurer’s providing 
coverage  meet s the acceptable insurer rating requirements in Section 31.4.  For Captives or 
RRGs that are not satisfactorily rated, the Seller /Servicer must review information  such as that 
listed below, as applicable, and assess the entity’s financial stability and ability to cover its 
projected loss  obligations , plus address  any non -compliance issues.  The Seller /Servicer must 
submit a waiver request along with a written assessment describing the results of its assessment  
and rationale for its recommendation to accept t he Captive or RRG.  Types of information to 
review include, but are not limited to, the following:  
 
• Certificate of Authority showing its state -issued license to conduct business including complete 
entity name, date of authority, and State of domicile  
 
• State Examination Report covering a specific time  frame and addressing a review of the 
entity’s balance sheet, income, investments, premiums, reinsurance, and loss history, as the 
entity’s financial condition, ability to fulfill its obligations, and compliance with applicable State 
laws and regulations  
 
• Actuarial Report that includes a statement of opinion after evaluating, opining, and certifying 
the adequacy of the entity’s open and paid losses, loss adjustment expense reserves, capital 
and surplus  
 
• The entity’s status as either a single -parent Captive, where an insurance subsidiary insures 
the loss exposures of its parent company or single entity, or a group Captive owned by several 
different companies that are normally from the same industry and have similar risks  
 
• Fronting insurer and/or reinsurance arrangements that support a recommendation
