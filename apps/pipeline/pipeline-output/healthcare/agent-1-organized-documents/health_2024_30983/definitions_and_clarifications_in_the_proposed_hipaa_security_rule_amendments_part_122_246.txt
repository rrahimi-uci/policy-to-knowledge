Given the increased portability of 
devices, media, workstations, and information systems, such components may often be located outside of a regulated entity’s physical location. For example, OCR has investigated several incidents involving portable electronic media and mobile workstations that were removed from the regulated entity’s physical environment and subsequently lost. As a result, the Department believes that we should more broadly construe the physical environment where ePHI is stored and accessed because it is essential that 
regulated entities have policies and procedures in place to address the portability of components of their information systems, as well as the ability of workforce members to access such information systems offsite using portable workstations. 
Additionally, the standard for device 
and media controls at 45 CFR 164.310(d)(1) applies only to devices and media, rather than all technology assets that may be components of a regulated entity’s relevant electronic information systems. The Department is concerned that a regulated entity may have other types of technology assets that may either create, receive, maintain, or transmit ePHI or otherwise affect its confidentiality, integrity, or availability and that can be removed from, brought to, or moved within its facilities. The confidentiality, integrity, or availability of the regulated entity’s ePHI could be negatively affected in the absence of written policies and procedures governing the movement of such technology assets. 
Finally, we believe that it is important 
to address several issues in the standards and implementation specifications for the physical safeguards that are also addressed in other proposals: addressing the Department’s expectations regarding implementation specifications;
