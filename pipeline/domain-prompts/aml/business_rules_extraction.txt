AML/BSA RULE CATEGORIES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Extract rules across these 10 categories:

1. **eligibility**: Who/what triggers AML obligations (customer types, transaction types, risk tiers)
2. **constraint**: Numeric limits and thresholds ($10,000 CTR, $5,000 SAR, 25% beneficial ownership)
3. **compliance**: Adherence requirements to BSA, FinCEN, FATF, OFAC, EU AMLD6

5. **documentation**: Required records, SAR/CTR filings, retention periods (5 years)

8. **reporting**: Mandatory SAR, CTR, and regulatory filing obligations
9. **monitoring**: Transaction monitoring alert generation and review
10. **screening**: OFAC, PEP, adverse media screening procedures

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
KEY AML/BSA THRESHOLDS AND DEADLINES (Reference Guide)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

**Reporting Thresholds:**
- CTR: Cash transactions ≥$10,000 (single or aggregated in 24-hour window); file within 15 calendar days
- SAR: Known/identified suspect: ≥$5,000; Unknown suspect: ≥$25,000; file within 30 calendar days (60 if no suspect identified)
- FBAR: Foreign financial accounts with aggregate value >$10,000; file by April 15 annually
- Monetary instruments: Purchases between $3,000–$10,000 must be recorded in BSA log

**Beneficial Ownership:**
- Control prong: Individual with significant managerial control (1 person per legal entity)
- Ownership prong: Natural persons owning ≥25% (up to 4 persons)
- EDD trigger: ≥10% ownership for high-risk customers

**Record Retention:**
- Transaction records: 5 years from transaction date
- Account records: 5 years from account closing
- SAR records: 5 years from filing date
- CTR records: 5 years from filing date
- CDD records: 5 years from account closing

**KYC/CDD Refresh Cycles:**
- HIGH risk customers: Annual CDD review
- MEDIUM risk customers: Biennial (every 2 years) CDD review
- LOW risk customers: Every 3–5 years CDD review
- Event-driven review: triggered by suspicious activity, adverse media, sanctions match
