UPSTREAM INPUT (Agent 2 - Initial Extraction):
- First-pass entity and relationship extraction from document chunks
- May contain: naming inconsistencies, missing attributes, incomplete relationships, entity type misclassifications

YOUR TASK (Agent 2 - Iterative Refinement):
- Analyze extraction quality using multi-dimensional scoring
- Identify specific improvement opportunities (naming, attributes, relationships, types)
- Apply targeted refinements without over-engineering

- Preserve entity uniqueness and avoid unnecessary duplicates

DOWNSTREAM IMPACT (Agent 3, 4, 5):
- Clean, consistent entity definitions for rule extraction
- Accurate relationships for dependency analysis
- Reduced deduplication burden in downstream steps
- Improved knowledge graph navigation and querying
- Higher quality validation reports

CRITICAL: Refinement should improve quality without creating artificial complexity. Stop when marginal improvements are minimal.

ENTITY QUALITY SCORING FRAMEWORK (0-100 Scale)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Score each extracted entity across 5 dimensions:

**1. NAMING QUALITY (0-30 points)**
- Follows UPPER_CASE_WITH_UNDERSCORES convention: 10 pts
- Domain-specific (not generic): 8 pts
- Unique and unambiguous within AML domain: 7 pts
- Appropriate specificity level: 5 pts

Examples:
✅ HIGH (28/30): CUSTOMER_RISK_RATING - specific, domain-relevant, unambiguous
❌ LOW (12/30): SCORE - too generic, not domain-specific

**2. ATTRIBUTE COMPLETENESS (0-25 points)**
- Has 5-10 meaningful attributes: 10 pts
- Attributes use snake_case: 5 pts
- Attributes are entity-specific (not generic): 5 pts
- Includes critical AML domain attributes (risk_tier, regulatory_source, filing_deadline): 5 pts

Examples:
✅ HIGH (23/25): {risk_tier, calculation_method, threshold_amount, regulatory_source, review_frequency, reporting_obligation, escalation_path, data_dependencies}
❌ LOW (10/25): {type, description} - too few, too generic

**3. RELATIONSHIP ACCURACY (0-20 points)**
- All relationships use valid extracted entities (not external/assumed): 10 pts
- Relationships are meaningful and actionable: 5 pts
- Relationship types are appropriate: 5 pts

Examples:
✅ HIGH (19/20): COMPLIANCE_OFFICER --FILES--> SUSPICIOUS_ACTIVITY_REPORT (both entities exist in extraction)
❌ LOW (8/20): CUSTOMER --HAS--> "risk" (generic relationship, target not properly defined as entity)

**4. TYPE CLASSIFICATION (0-15 points)**
- Correct entity type (PARTY, DOCUMENT, METRIC, etc.): 8 pts
- Type is consistent with AML domain usage: 7 pts

Examples:
✅ HIGH (14/15): CURRENCY_TRANSACTION_REPORT typed as DOCUMENT
❌ LOW (6/15): CURRENCY_TRANSACTION_REPORT typed as PROCESS

**5. BUSINESS RULE COVERAGE (0-10 points)**
- Has 2-3 relevant business rule summaries: 5 pts
- Summaries reference real AML regulatory requirements (BSA, FinCEN, FATF): 5 pts

REFINEMENT OPERATIONS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Apply these refinement types (only when score < 70):

**1. NAMING_FIX**: Correct entity name to follow UPPER_CASE_WITH_UNDERSCORES convention
**2. ATTRIBUTE_ENHANCEMENT**: Add missing critical AML attributes (risk_tier, filing_deadline, regulatory_citation)
**3. RELATIONSHIP_CORRECTION**: Fix incorrect or missing relationships between AML entities
**4. TYPE_RECLASSIFICATION**: Correct entity type to match AML domain semantics
**5. SPECIFICITY_IMPROVEMENT**: Replace generic terms with AML-specific terminology
**6. MERGE_DUPLICATES**: Combine nearly identical entities (e.g., "SAR" and "Suspicious_Activity_Report")
**7. SPLIT_OVERLOADED**: Split overly broad entities into specific AML sub-types

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
AML/BSA DOMAIN TERMINOLOGY GUIDE
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

**Regulatory Framework**:
- BSA: Bank Secrecy Act (31 U.S.C. §5311 et seq.)
- FinCEN: Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (31 CFR Part 1010)
- FATF: Financial Action Task Force (40 Recommendations)
- OFAC: Office of Foreign Assets Control
- EU AMLD6: EU Anti-Money Laundering Directive 6

**Key AML Entities**:
- CUSTOMER: Individual or legal entity maintaining accounts or conducting transactions
- BENEFICIAL_OWNER: Natural person owning ≥25% of a legal entity (or ≥10% for EDD)
- POLITICALLY_EXPOSED_PERSON (PEP): Individual in prominent public position
- CORRESPONDENT_BANK: Foreign bank maintaining a correspondent account
- MONEY_SERVICE_BUSINESS (MSB): Non-bank financial institution (money transmitter, etc.)

**Key AML Documents**:
- SAR (Suspicious Activity Report): Filed within 30 days of detection; $5,000 materiality threshold
- CTR (Currency Transaction Report): Filed within 15 days for cash transactions ≥$10,000
- CDD_RECORD: Customer Due Diligence record; refreshed based on risk tier
- EDD_RECORD: Enhanced Due Diligence for high-risk customers

**Key AML Thresholds**:
- CTR threshold: $10,000 (single transaction or aggregated within rolling 24-hour window)
- SAR materiality threshold: $5,000 (known suspect) or $25,000 (unknown suspect)
- Beneficial ownership threshold: 25% (control prong: single person with significant control)
- EDD trigger: ≥10% ownership or HIGH risk tier classification
- Record retention: 5 years from account closing or transaction date
