════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
RULE TYPE TAXONOMY (10 Categories)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Classify each rule into exactly ONE of these 10 categories using the **rule_type** field:

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
THE 10 RULE TYPES (Mutually Exclusive)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. **eligibility** - Who/what qualifies
   Detection: "eligible", "qualifies", "must be", borrower/property requirements
   Examples:
   - "Borrower must have credit score ≥ 620"
   - "Property must be owner-occupied primary residence"
   - "Loan must be for purchase or rate-term refinance"

2. **constraint** - Numeric limits and thresholds
   Detection: "≤", "≥", "maximum", "minimum", "limit", "cap", "floor"
   Examples:
   - "Maximum LTV 97% for first-time buyers"
   - "DTI cannot exceed 43% for QM loans"
   - "Conforming loan limit $726,200 single-family"

3. **calculation** - Formulas and computations
   Detection: "calculated as", "equals", "ratio of", "sum of", "formula"
   Examples:
   - "DTI = (Monthly Debt / Gross Monthly Income) × 100"
   - "LTV = (Loan Amount / Appraised Value) × 100"
   - "PITI = Principal + Interest + Taxes + Insurance"

4. **validation** - Verification requirements
   Detection: "verified", "confirmed", "validated", "documented", "evidenced"
   Examples:
   - "Income verified via VOE or 2 years tax returns"
   - "Employment confirmed within 10 days of closing"
   - "Identity validated through government-issued ID"

5. **process** - Procedural steps and timing
   Detection: "within X days", "before", "after", "prior to", "sequence"
   Examples:
   - "Appraisal completed within 120 days of closing"
   - "CD delivered 3 business days before closing"
   - "Title search completed before final approval"

6. **compliance** - Regulatory requirements
   Detection: "required by", "per regulation", "TRID", "RESPA", "QM"
   Examples:
   - "TRID requires CD delivery 3 days before closing"
   - "QM loans must meet ATR requirements"
   - "RESPA requires good faith estimates"

7. **documentation** - Required forms and records
   Detection: "form", "document", "record", "file", "certification"
   Examples:
   - "Form 1003 URLA required for all applications"
   - "4506-C required for IRS transcript"
   - "Hazard insurance certificate required at closing"

8. **prohibition** - Things explicitly NOT allowed
   Detection: "cannot", "must not", "prohibited", "not allowed", "forbidden", "never"
   Examples:
   - "Cash-out refinance cannot exceed 80% CLTV"
   - "Kickbacks for referrals are prohibited under RESPA"
   - "Negative amortization not allowed for QM loans"
   - "Borrower cannot have bankruptcy within 4 years"

9. **definition** - What terms and concepts mean
   Detection: "defined as", "means", "is considered", "refers to", "classified as"
   Examples:
   - "Primary residence = property occupied by borrower ≥ 60% of year"
   - "First-time homebuyer = no ownership interest in past 3 years"
   - "Conforming loan = meets GSE purchase criteria"
   - "ARM = loan with interest rate that adjusts periodically"

10. **exception** - Waivers, overrides, and special cases
    Detection: "except when", "unless", "waiver", "override", "special case", "compensating factors"
    Examples:
    - "DTI may exceed 43% with compensating factors"
    - "Manual underwrite can override DU findings with documentation"
    - "LTV limits waived for VA loans"
    - "Exception granted for borrowers with 12+ months reserves"

EXTRACTION QUALITY STANDARDS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Each business rule MUST include:

✓ **rule_id**: GLOBALLY UNIQUE identifier with batch number (BR_ENTITYNAME_TYPE_[BATCHNUM]_001)
✓ **rule_name**: GLOBALLY UNIQUE human-readable natural language title with specific thresholds — no underscores, no CamelCase (30-60 chars)
✓ **rule_type**: One of 10 types: eligibility|constraint|calculation|validation|process|compliance|documentation|prohibition|definition|exception
✓ **description**: A clear, complete explanation of the rule, including specific numeric thresholds or constraints where applicable.
✓ **conditions**: When/where rule applies (40-100 chars)
✓ **consequences**: What happens if met/violated (40-100 chars)
✓ **exceptions**: Special cases, waivers, or N/A (20-80 chars)
✓ **source_reference**: A structured object pinpointing the exact location of the rule in the source chunk:
  - chunk_path (required): Relative path to the source chunk file — MUST match the FILE path from the input documents exactly (e.g., "FAMA/FAMA_001.txt")
  - section_id (required): Document section identifier if available, or "N/A" (e.g., "B2-1-01, Part A.2.3")
  - start_word_position (required): 0-based word offset where the rule text begins within the chunk content (count words from the start of the chunk)
  - end_word_position (required): 0-based word offset where the rule text ends (exclusive)
  - source_text (required): The verbatim excerpt (30-150 words) from the chunk that this rule was derived from. Must be a direct quote, not paraphrased.
  HOW TO COMPUTE WORD POSITIONS: Split the chunk content on whitespace. Count from 0. The start_word_position is the index of the first word of the relevant passage. The end_word_position is the index after the last word. The source_text should match content.split()[start_word_position:end_word_position] approximately.
✓ **mandatory**: true (required) or false (optional/conditional)
✓ **examples**: 2-3 concrete, realistic scenarios
✓ **confidence_score**: Overall confidence 0-100 (see scoring rubric below)
✓ **confidence_breakdown**: Detailed scores for each factor
✓ **effective_date**: Date the rule became effective in ISO 8601 format (YYYY-MM-DD), or null if not stated in the document
✓ **expiration_date**: Date the rule expires or was superseded (YYYY-MM-DD), or null if still active
✓ **superseded_by**: rule_id of the replacement rule, or null if this rule is current
✓ **jurisdiction**: Scope of authority. One of: "federal" | "state:<XX>" (e.g., "state:CA") | "agency:FNMA" | "agency:FHLMC" | "agency:FHA" | "agency:VA" | "lender_overlay"
✓ **risk_level**: Business impact if violated. One of: "critical" | "high" | "medium" | "low"
✓ **related_rules**: Array of rule_ids that interact with this rule (dependencies, conflicts, prerequisites). Empty array [] if none known.
✓ **enforcement_action**: Specific consequence on violation (e.g., "loan repurchase demand", "indemnification", "suspension", "fine"). Use "N/A" if not applicable.
✓ **applicability_scope**: Structured object specifying when the rule applies:
  - loan_types: Array of applicable loan types (e.g., ["conventional", "FHA", "VA", "USDA"])
  - occupancy_types: Array of occupancy types (e.g., ["primary_residence", "second_home", "investment"])
  - transaction_types: Array of transaction types (e.g., ["purchase", "rate_term_refinance", "cash_out_refinance"])
✓ **data_points_required**: Array of specific data fields needed to evaluate this rule (e.g., ["credit_score", "ltv_ratio", "dti_ratio", "property_type"])
✓ **audit_frequency**: How often this rule should be re-verified. One of: "at_origination" | "monthly" | "quarterly" | "annually" | "on_change"

CONFIDENCE SCORING RUBRIC (NEW - CRITICAL FOR QUALITY)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

For EVERY rule, assess confidence using this 5-factor rubric:

**1. EXTRACTION CLARITY (0-100):**
- 90-100: Explicit "must/shall/required" language with clear thresholds
- 70-89: Clear requirement using descriptive language
- 50-69: Implied requirement inferred from context
- Below 50: Ambiguous or uncertain extraction

**2. NUMERIC PRECISION (0-100):**
- 100: Exact numbers stated ("≥ 620", "80% LTV", "$726,200")
- 75: Range provided ("620-640", "between 80-97%")
- 50: Approximate ("around 620", "approximately 80%")
- 25: Qualitative only ("good credit", "sufficient funds")
- 0: No numeric thresholds in rule

**3. CONTEXT COMPLETENESS (0-100):**
- 90-100: All conditions, consequences, and exceptions clearly stated
- 70-89: Main conditions clear, minor gaps in exceptions
- 50-69: Conditions stated but consequences or exceptions unclear
- Below 50: Incomplete context, significant ambiguity

**4. SOURCE AUTHORITY (0-100):**
- 100: Direct quote or paraphrase from official regulatory section
- 80: Clear derivation from primary source document
- 60: Inferred from multiple related sections
- 40: Derived from examples or implicit requirements
- Below 40: Based on domain knowledge, not document text

**5. LOGICAL CONSISTENCY (0-100):**
- 100: Rule is internally consistent, no contradictions
- 80: Minor terminology variations but logically sound
- 60: Some ambiguity in conditions or consequences
- Below 60: Logical gaps or potential contradictions

**CONFIDENCE_SCORE CALCULATION:**
Overall confidence = weighted average:
- Extraction Clarity: 30%
- Numeric Precision: 25%
- Context Completeness: 20%
- Source Authority: 15%
- Logical Consistency: 10%
